About my Drawings

Drawing has been a hobby of mine since the age of 6 years old. My uncle introduced me to the process of drawing an illustration, which grew my love for art overall.

What is Drawing?

Drawing is the outward expression of the mind's creativity through means of illustration and digital media.

When do I Draw?

My creative juices begin to flow mostly in the midst of the night. Late-night drawings are always the best times for me to produce my best work. During the night I have the feeling of freedom and no judgement, thus sparking my creative mindset to craft something new and cool.

Where to Draw?

You can draw anywhere! From your home to nature, inspiration is everywhere. My favorite place to draw is at desk. This gives me stability to place all of my art material a stable surface to draw on.

Often great artist work around cluttered desks because only they are able to see their vision in the mess they make. A messy desk to me means that the artist is crafting a beautiful vision onto paper. Typically when my desk is cluttered my mind begins to focus on my main objective for my illustration.

How to Draw?

Start with basic shapes such as circles and triangles and form them into the anatomy you want. From there add connect these shapes in drawing with lines to form the body being drawn. After connecting the shapes procede to add detail such as shading and specific details that you want. In my experience when drawing something you always want to use the process of "construction", meaning to break down complex ideas into simple forms or shapes. Also take into account proportions of items being drawn, create some kind of vanishing point on your page.

Why do I Draw?

My reason for drawing is based on my influence as a kid and my respect for the process of drawing as I continue to study and learn new techniques. Some of my biggest inspirations comes from artists such as the late Kim Jung Gi, Todd Mcfarlen, Takehiko Inoue, and the likes of Chase Conley. Becoming a student of the process has taught me a new respect for the craft overall, and a new found love for the process of drawing arises each time a draw something new.
